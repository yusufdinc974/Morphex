About Morphix

Morphix is a university-level project developed as part of the "Computer Engineering Project" course at Gebze Technical University. Our main goal is to design a low-cost and accessible system that allows real-world objects to be modeled in 3D and transferred into virtual environments, such as video games.

Unlike expensive professional 3D scanning systems that use LiDAR or radar sensors, Morphix creates a complete scanning and modeling system using only simple components: a standard camera, LED light, laser line, and stepper motor - all controlled by a Raspberry Pi. Our system operates automatically inside a custom-built scanning box, capturing both color and geometry data to create game-ready 3D models.

Team RealityShapers consists of eight third-year Computer Engineering students who collaborated across hardware design, data processing, and game integration to make this innovative project a reality.

How Morphix Works

Our innovative 3D scanning pipeline combines hardware automation with advanced image processing to transform physical objects into game-ready 3D models. The entire process is controlled by a Raspberry Pi and executed inside a custom-built scanning chamber.

01

Object Placement & Setup

Place the physical object on our precision rotating platform inside the custom scanning box. The platform is driven by a NEMA-17 stepper motor with 0.1125Â° step precision, ensuring accurate 360Â° rotation. The object undergoes two separate scanning passes for optimal data capture.

Object on rotating platform before scanning

02

Dual-Pass Image Capture

Color Pass: High-brightness white LED illuminates the object while the Raspberry Pi Camera V3 captures 200 color images during full rotation, ensuring comprehensive texture coverage.

Laser Pass: Red line laser projects horizontal lines across the object's surface while capturing 200 laser images, detecting precise silhouettes for 3D geometry reconstruction.

03

Advanced Image Processing

Using OpenCV for laser line detection and image enhancement, our system processes the captured data through sophisticated algorithms. Point Cloud Library (PCL) converts detected laser points into 3D point clouds, then applies Poisson surface reconstruction to generate smooth, accurate meshes with proper texture mapping.

04

Game Integration & Export

Final 3D models are exported in multiple formats (OBJ, PLY, FBX) using the Assimp library, ensuring compatibility with Unreal Engine. Models are automatically imported into our game environment as interactive obstacles or collectible items, complete with physics properties and collision detection for seamless gameplay integration.
